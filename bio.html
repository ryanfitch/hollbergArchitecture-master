ABOUT


HOLLBERG ARCHITECTURE IS CENTERED ON THE RELATIONSHIP OF CONSTRUCTABILITY AND CREATIVITY. IT IS LED BY BENJAMIN HOLLBERG, WHO HOLDS A BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN ART FROM THE UNIVERSITY OF OREGON AND A MASTERS OF ARCHITECTURE WITH DEPARTMENTAL HONORS FROM PARSONS THE NEW SCHOOL FOR DESIGN. BENJAMIN IS A REGISTERED ARCHITECT LICENSED IN THE STATE OF NEW YORK AND A LEED ACCREDITED PROFESSIONAL WITH A SPECIALTY IN BUILDING DESIGN AND CONSTRUCTION. HE HAS TAUGHT ARCHITECTURE COURSES AT PARSONS THE NEW SCHOOL OF DESIGN AND HONG KONG UNIVERSITY, AND HAS BEEN A GUEST JUROR AT A NUMBER OF UNIVERSITIES AROUND THE UNITED STATES AND HONG KONG. HE HAS ENGAGED IN COLLABORATIVE RESEARCH DEVELOPING NEW METHODS OF ANALYZING CLASSICAL ARCHITECTURE AND, SEPARATELY, STUDIED THE DENSITY OF PRIVATE HOUSING ESTATES IN HONG KONG. HIS BUILT WORK RANGES FROM MIXED-USE CONDOS, GROUND-UP TOWNHOUSES, OFFICE FITOUTS, RETAIL DESIGN, SUPPORTIVE HOUSING COMPLEXES, CUSTOM FURNITURE, AND EDUCATION FACILITIES. HIS WORK HAS BEEN PUBLISHED IN THE NEW YORK TIMES AND ARCHITECTURE RECORD AND FEATURED IN NUMEROUS GALLERIES. BENJAMIN IS AN EAGLE SCOUT, HAS PREVIOUSLY WORKED AS A PROFESSIONAL CHEF, IS A GRADUATE OF AMERICORPS, AND HOLDS A CERTIFICATE FROM THE PACIFIC NORTHWEST HISTORIC PRESERVATION FIELD SCHOOL.
